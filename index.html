The Simplest Mental Model

Commercialization isn’t “selling ads”. It’s turning attention + emotion into measurable business value.

Sports Commercialization = (Audience × Emotion × Access) → Monetization

Audience

  • How many people, who are they
  • Minutes watched matters
  • Where they come from (app, telco, social)

Emotion

  • Derby / rivalry spikes attention
  • Live moments are less skippable
  • Identity + community = premium value

Access

  • OTT usability + pricing + bundles
  • Discoverability is product
  • Availability drives reach

Monetization

  • Rights / Ads / Sponsors
  • Subscriptions (event-led spikes)
  • Data, partnerships, retention
Ask: “Why does a derby sell higher than a normal match?” → because emotion makes attention deeper and harder to skip.

The Sports Value Chain

Who pays whom — and where BLQ creates leverage.

Key idea: Rights aren’t expensive. Uncertainty is expensive. BLQ’s job is to reduce uncertainty with packaging + distribution + measurement.
Ask: “Who carries the risk?” → OTT/Broadcaster. Then: “So what makes us win?” → plan + measurement + packaging.

What We Actually Sell: Inventory

Commercialization starts when we define inventory, quantify it, and package it.

Rights Inventory

SCARCE
  • Exclusive / non-exclusive digital
  • Linear + OTT + highlights + clips
  • Catch-up / VOD / delayed
  • Territory + exclusivity drives price

Ad Inventory

UNITS
  • Pre / mid / post-roll
  • Overlays, bugs, L-band
  • Studio segments
  • Units × reach becomes revenue

Sponsorship Assets

OWNERSHIP
  • Title sponsor / powered-by
  • “Best Play”, “Player of Match” moments
  • Category exclusivity
  • Brands buy association + story

Digital + Data

FLYWHEEL
  • Social clips + creators
  • Registrations, CRM, retention
  • Bundles: telco / ISP / device
  • Measure → report → renew → reprice
Mindset shift: Stop saying “we’ll sell sponsorship” → start saying “we have X inventory + Y moments + Z measurable outcomes.”
Quick exercise: pick one event and ask the room to list 10 inventory items we can sell.

The 4 Revenue Engines

Most real deals are bundles — not a single revenue line.

Nepal-market truth: subscription spikes are event-led. We must design retention (bundles, follow-up content, offers) to keep value after the event.
If rights fee is high, we must build a stronger mix: sponsors + subs + ads. “One engine rarely wins alone.”

Packaging: How Deals Are Built

A deal is a promise: audience + distribution + moments + measurement + exclusivity.

The 5 Promises

  • Audience: reach + minutes watched + audience profile
  • Distribution: where it appears + how discoverable it is
  • Integration: what the brand gets to own (moments)
  • Measurement: what we report + proof of delivery
  • Exclusivity: category locks that justify premium pricing
Sell moments, not minutes. Emotion is the premium.

Packaging Canvas (Quick Template)

  • Event: ____________
  • Audience promise: ____________
  • Distribution: OTT / Linear / Social / Bundles
  • Moments: Title, segments, awards, overlays
  • Exclusivity: Category = ____________
  • Measurement: Reach, watch time, conversions
Line to remember:
“A sponsor doesn’t buy logo space. They buy permission to borrow fandom.”
Ask the room: pick one upcoming property and fill the canvas in 60 seconds.

Pricing: 3 Defensible Anchors

Pricing is a story backed by evidence — not just a number.

1) Cost-plus

  • Rights fee + production + marketing
  • + margin based on risk
  • Best when cost structure is clear
Use when: you need a safe baseline.

2) CPM benchmark

  • Impressions × CPM equivalent
  • Premium multiplier for live + key moments
  • Best for ad-heavy packages
Use when: you can estimate delivery.

3) Outcome-based

  • Installs, trials, subs, leads
  • Performance bonus tiers
  • Best for digital-first brands
Use when: conversion tracking is strong.

Reality check

  • Exclusivity increases value sharply
  • Moments price higher than inventory
  • Measurement = credibility = higher renewal pricing
Rule: Package first, price second.
If someone asks “what CPM?” answer: CPM is a reference; we price emotion + exclusivity + delivery proof.

Measurement & Reporting

Measurement is how we turn a one-time deal into a renewal — and a higher price next time.

Must-report metrics

  • Reach: unique viewers / unique users
  • Engagement: minutes watched, avg watch time
  • Quality: completion rate, peak concurrency (if available)
  • Conversion: installs, trials, subs, leads
  • Proof: delivery logs, screenshots, ad reports
Non-negotiable: reporting format + frequency should be agreed in the contract.

The Commercialization Flywheel

Measure → Report → Trust → Renew → Reprice

The better we report, the easier the next deal becomes.

Line to remember:
“What we can measure, we can sell again — at a higher price.”
Emphasize: measurement is the difference between “broker” and “partner”. It reduces uncertainty for brands.

Commercialization Is a Team Sport

Revenue happens when every function plays their part — not only “sales”.

Who owns what

  • Rights: valuation, negotiation, contract terms
  • Programming: schedule, positioning, content fit
  • Production: quality, cost control, brand safety
  • Sales: sponsors, ad packages, renewals
  • Marketing: demand generation, conversion
  • Data/Tech: measurement, attribution, retention
  • Finance/Legal: risk, compliance, invoicing

Operating rule

Rights are the fuel.
Distribution is the engine.
Measurement is the dashboard.

Internal question

“Which part do I own that directly increases revenue or renewal probability?”

Make it personal: ask each team member to say which part they own and one improvement they can make next event.

5 Rules of the Game

  1. Scarcity sells (limited matches, limited exclusivity)
  2. Packaging beats pricing
  3. Distribution is product (UX + access)
  4. Sponsors buy stories, not spots
  5. Measurement builds pricing power
Final line: Our job is to turn passion into predictable revenue.
