What We Actually Sell: Inventory

Commercialization starts when we clearly define what we can sell, how scarce it is, and how we measure it.

Rights Inventory

  • Exclusive/non-exclusive digital
  • Linear + OTT + highlights + clips
  • Catch-up / VOD / delayed

Ad Inventory

  • Pre/mid/post-roll
  • Overlays, bugs, L-band
  • Segments (studio, analysis)

Sponsorship Assets

  • Title sponsor, powered-by
  • “Player of Match”, “Best Play”
  • Category exclusivity

Digital + Data

  • Social clips + creators
  • Registrations, CRM, retention
  • Bundles: telco / ISP / device
Shift in mindset: stop saying “we’ll sell sponsorship” → start saying “we have X units of inventory and Y high-impact moments.”

Packaging: How a Deal Is Built

What we promise

  • Audience: reach + minutes watched
  • Distribution: where it appears + discoverability
  • Integration: moments the brand can own
  • Measurement: reports + proof of delivery
  • Exclusivity: category locks

Core principle

“A sponsor doesn’t buy logo space. They buy permission to borrow fandom.”

Packaging beats pricing. Measurement builds pricing power.

Tip: Sell “moments” (high emotion) more than “minutes”.
Explain category exclusivity and why it increases value massively for certain brands.

Pricing: 3 Defensible Anchors

1) Cost-plus

  • Rights fee + production + marketing
  • Add margin based on risk

2) CPM benchmark

  • Impressions × CPM equivalent
  • Adjust for live & premium moments

3) Outcome-based

  • Installs, trials, subs, leads
  • Performance bonus tiers

Reality check

  • Pricing is a story backed by evidence
  • Evidence = audience history + market benchmarks + delivery plan
If asked “what CPM?” say: we benchmark but we price moments + exclusivity, not just impressions.

Measurement & Reporting (How We Prove Value)

Must report

  • Reach: unique viewers
  • Engagement: minutes watched, completion
  • Conversion: installs, trials, subs
  • Delivery proof: logs, screenshots

Why it matters

“What we can measure, we can sell again — at a higher price.”

Commercialization flywheel: Measure → Report → Trust → Renew → Higher pricing
Mention that measurement is how BLQ becomes “premium” vs generic brokers.

Commercialization Is a Team Sport

Who does what

  • Rights: valuation + negotiation
  • Programming: fit + scheduling
  • Production: quality + cost control
  • Sales: ads + sponsors
  • Marketing: demand + conversion
  • Data/Tech: measurement + retention
  • Finance/Legal: risk + contracting

Working rule

“Rights are the fuel. Distribution is the engine. Measurement is the dashboard.”

BLQ advantage: we can connect rights → distribution → brand outcomes in one plan.
Ask the team: “Which part do you own?” Make it personal.

5 Rules of the Game

  1. Scarcity sells (limited matches, limited exclusivity)
  2. Packaging beats pricing
  3. Distribution is product (UX + access)
  4. Sponsors buy stories, not spots
  5. Measurement builds pricing power
